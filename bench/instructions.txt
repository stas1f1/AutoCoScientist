You are participating in Autods-bench, an offline version of Kaggle competitions adapted to test the coding ability of agents like yourself.

You will be given a machine learning task. You must solve the task by training a model and running the model on the test set to produce a submission file. You may not hand-label the data to produce a submission file.

BENCHMARK INSTRUCTIONS
------
First, please read the instructions below which are specific to our benchmark setup.

- **Competition-specific instructions**: The instructions for the competition are available in `description.md`. Read this file to understand the problem setup for the competition you are currently solving.
- **Dataset**: The dataset files for the competition are available in your working directory.
- **Submission (final, no changes after)**: We have simplified all competition types to use a simple CSV submission format. Regardless of what the competition instructions say, you MUST produce a submission file at `submission.csv`. We will only evaluate submissions found at this path; failure to submit a `submission.csv` at this path will result in a failed attempt. **Once `submission.csv` is written, your attempt is final—no further code changes, file edits, or re-submissions are allowed.** Do not write `submission.csv` until you believe the result reflects your best achievable performance within the limits.
- **Model quality (baseline is not the finish line)**: You may start from a simple baseline to validate the pipeline, but you MUST NOT stop there. Your mandate is to produce the most performant model you are capable of building within the compute, time, and step limits. Continue iterating (features, models, tuning, ensembling, etc.) until further improvements are no longer achievable under these constraints.
- **Test set**: In general, since we do not have access to the original test labels, we have constructed our own train/test split for each competition. As such, the dataset you see in your working directory may differ slightly from what is described in the competition instructions. Nevertheless the files available to you should otherwise be similar to the original and you should proceed with the competition task on the train/test splits available to you.
- **Cheating**: You are not allowed to view others' solutions to this competition. If we detect that your submission is plagiarized from others, the submission will be disqualified.

Your submission will be graded based on the metric in the competition-specific instructions. Your final submission must demonstrate the best performance you are capable of achieving under the given compute, time, and step constraints.

Wherever there is a conflict between the points above and the competition-specific instructions in `description.md`, the above points should always take priority since these are always true for our offline benchmark setup.

ADDITIONAL NOTES
------

- **Compute**: You have access to a CPU with the appropriate drivers installed.
